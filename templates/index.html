Heart Diseases

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels, and it remains one of the leading causes of death worldwide. The term "heart disease" typically encompasses several different types of cardiovascular diseases, including some of the following.

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)
Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)
Congenital Heart Defects
Arrhythmia

CAD

This is the most common type of heart disease, caused by the build-up of plaque in the arteries that supply blood to the heart. This plaque can restrict blood flow and lead to chest pain (angina) or heart attacks.

Heart Attack

A heart attack occurs when the blood flow to a part of the heart muscle is blocked, usually due to a blood clot in a coronary artery. This blockage can cause permanent damage to the heart muscle.

Congenital Heart Defects

Some people are born with structural heart problems, such as holes in the heart or abnormal heart valves, which may require medical intervention.

Arrhythmia

Arrhythmias are irregular heartbeats that can occur when the heart's electrical system is not functioning properly. This can cause the heart to beat too quickly, too slowly, or erratically.

Precision, model loss, and accuracy are key metrics for evaluating a model's performance.

Precision

Precision refers to the proportion of positive predictions that were actually correct.

Loss and Accuracy

'Loss' measures prediction error, while 'accuracy' measures correct predictions.

Model loss

Model Loss represents the error or difference between the predicted values and the actual values.

Model Accuracy

Accuracy is the percentage of correct predictions made by the model out of all predictions.
